# 1. Drum Design: The Heart of the System

The drum is not just a rotating cylinder.

Its design determines:

* Heat transfer balance
* Bean movement
* Development uniformity
* Energy retention

## Key Engineering Variables

Professional drum design depends on several factors:

* Drum diameter
* Drum length
* Material thickness
* Perforation structure (if any)
* Rotation speed

A larger diameter does not automatically mean better roasting.

The ratio between drum diameter and batch capacity must be engineered correctly.

This is why professional machines behave differently from lighter, entry-level units.

---

# 2. Heat Transfer: Three Forces Working Together

Professional coffee roasters rely on three heat transfer methods:

* Conduction (direct contact with the drum)
* Convection (hot air movement)
* Radiation (infrared heat energy)

A well-engineered commercial machine balances these forces carefully.

Too much conduction can scorch beans.

Too much airflow without proper energy can lead to flat development.

Professional systems are designed to allow controlled interaction between these heat sources throughout the roast.

---

# 3. Burner System Engineering

In gas-powered commercial machines, burner design is critical.

It determines:

* Heat responsiveness
* Energy efficiency
* Flame stability
* Safety

## What Professional Burners Must Deliver

Industrial burners must provide:

* Fast response to adjustments
* Stable flame under varying airflow conditions
* Even heat distribution across the drum

Weak burner systems cause slow heat recovery between batches, directly impacting production efficiency.

In professional roasting environments, consistency across multiple consecutive batches is non-negotiable.

# 4. Airflow and Pressure Management

Airflow is often underestimated.

In reality, airflow determines:

* Moisture removal
* Chaff evacuation
* Rate of rise control
* Clean cup clarity

## Why Airflow Matters

Professional coffee roasters include:

* Adjustable airflow systems
* Strong industrial fans
* Stable pressure management
* Proper exhaust balancing

Poor airflow design can lead to:

* Smoky flavors
* Uneven development
* Heat instability

Engineering airflow correctly is every bit as important as burner design.

---

# 5. Thermal Stability and Batch Recovery

In commercial production, roasting is not one batch—it is continuous production.

After finishing a roast, the machine must:

* Discharge beans
* Recover heat
* Stabilize quickly
* Begin the next batch without temperature drift

## The Importance of Thermal Mass

Professional coffee roasters are engineered with sufficient thermal mass to prevent:

* Temperature crashes
* Overcompensation heating
* Profile inconsistency

This is one of the biggest differences between hobby-level and production-level machines.
